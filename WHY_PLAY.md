# Why play Quantum Maze Escape?

This page is for **players** (and anyone demoing the game): what you get from playing, in plain language.

---

## What is this game for?

**Quantum Maze Escape** is a **puzzle maze** with special rules inspired by ideas from **quantum physics**—not a quantum computer in your browser. The “use” of playing is a mix of **fun**, **mental challenge**, and **gentle learning**.

---

## If you want to play for fun

- **Clear goal:** reach the exit (★) on a grid maze.
- **Extra spice:** you can **split** into several positions at once, **measure** to collapse back to one, and use **portals** (matching symbols) to jump across the map.
- **Many stages:** six difficulty modes (Easy → Extreme), each with **100 levels**, so you can play in short sessions or grind for completion.
- **Progress:** your **clears are saved in this browser** per **name** and **difficulty**—you can see which stages you’ve finished in each mode.

---

## If you want to learn something

Playing helps you **remember metaphors** that teachers use for quantum concepts:

| Idea (simplified) | What you do in the game |
|-------------------|-------------------------|
| **Superposition** — “many possibilities at once” | **Split:** you exist in several cells as “ghosts” until you narrow things down. |
| **Measurement / collapse** — “one outcome when you ‘look’” | **Measure:** one position is kept at random; the rest disappear. |
| **Entanglement (metaphor)** — “linked behaviour between two places” | **Portals:** same Greek letter on two tiles — step on one, appear on the other. |

**Important:** the game is **not** doing real quantum math. It’s a **toy** that makes those *words* feel less abstract after you’ve moved, split, and collapsed a few times.

---

## If you want a quick mental workout

- **Planning:** when to split, when to measure, when to save charges.
- **Spatial thinking:** maze layout, portals, and the legend (start, exit, energy).
- **Focus:** harder modes mean bigger mazes and tighter resources.

---

## Who is it for?

- **Students** revising superposition / measurement / entanglement vocabulary before a quiz or viva.
- **Anyone** who likes maze games and wants a **sci‑fi** twist.
- **Demos:** teachers or presenters showing that **code + story** can explain dry topics.

---

## How to start (reminder)

1. Open `index.html` or run a tiny local server (see **README.md**).
2. Type a **name** (progress is tied to it on this device).
3. Pick **difficulty**, then **Enter the maze** or **Start game**.
4. Use **arrow keys** or **W A S D**; use **Split** and **Measure** from the side panel.
5. Open **Game rules** anytime from the title screen, mission brief, header, or pause menu.

---

## In one sentence

**You play to enjoy a challenging maze, and along the way you build an intuitive picture of superposition, collapse, and “linked” portals—useful for studying or explaining quantum *ideas*, not for running quantum algorithms.**
